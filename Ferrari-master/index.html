Enzo Ferrari

Exalted Race car driver who founded one of the most prestigous and distinguished car companies of all time.

Enzo Ferrari Story


Enzo Anselmo Ferrari was an Italian automotive engineer and entrepreneur, best known as the founder of the Ferrari automobile company. He was born on February 18, 1898, in Modena, Italy, and died on August 14, 1988, in Maranello, Italy, at the age of 90. Ferrari developed a passion for racing as a young man, and he began working for the Alfa Romeo racing team in 1920. He quickly became a prominent figure in the racing world and went on to found Scuderia Ferrari in 1929, which served as Alfa Romeo's racing division. In 1947, Ferrari launched his own car company, which he named Ferrari S.p.A. The company's first car was the Ferrari 125 S, which was powered by a 1.5-liter V12 engine. Ferrari's cars quickly gained a reputation for their speed and performance, and they became some of the most sought-after sports cars in the world. Enzo Ferrari was a fiercely competitive individual, and his dedication to racing and engineering excellence helped establish the Ferrari brand as a symbol of luxury, performance, and exclusivity. Throughout his life, Ferrari remained actively involved in the management of his company, and continued to design new cars well into his 80s. Today, Ferrari's brand remains one of the world's most recognizable and prestigious automotive brands, and Enzo Ferrari is remembered as one of the most important figures in the history of the automobile industry.
